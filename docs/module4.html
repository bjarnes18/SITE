Module 4: Reflection on Sprint 1

What Happened in Sprint 1?

During Sprint 1, I took Marketing, Accounting, and International Business Communication. I struggled with Marketing because I skipped classes, making studying harder. Fortunately, there were no external issues impacting my progress.

Course Reflections

Marketing: I felt it wouldn’t go well because I dislike theory, and my results confirmed this.

Accounting: Practical exercises and engaging lessons made this subject much more enjoyable and easier to succeed in.

Classmates and Teachers

  • Classmates: Their behavior was similar to mine, and it didn’t influence me.
  • Teachers: In Accounting, clear explanations and practical exercises helped me stay focused and make progress.

What Went Well?

Accounting went well because it was practical, engaging, and easier to study compared to theory-heavy subjects.

Challenges and Negative Effects

I struggled in Marketing due to my lack of motivation and effort to study theoretical material.

Plans for Improvement

To perform better in Sprint 2, I plan to:

  • Start studying earlier and stick to a schedule.
  • Set weekly goals to keep myself on track.

Taking these steps will help me stay motivated and perform better in subjects I find less interesting.

Summary: Lessons Learned

From my first experience in higher education, my advice to new students is:

Attend all classes, study consistently, and don’t neglect subjects that seem uninteresting. Start early, plan well, and stay on track.

Learning Question

How can I stay motivated to study subjects I find boring and still perform well in them?
