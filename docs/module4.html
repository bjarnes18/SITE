Module 4: Reflection on Sprint 1

Reflection on Sprint 1

What happened with regard to my studies in Sprint 1?
In Sprint 1, I took Marketing, Accounting, and International Business Communication. I struggled with Marketing because I skipped classes, which made studying harder. Outside of class, there were no issues that impacted my progress.

How did I feel about each course?
At the start, I felt Marketing wouldn’t go well because I dislike studying theory. I was right—my results reflected my lack of motivation. In contrast, Accounting went well as I enjoy practical exercises and found it interesting.

How did others react?
My classmates seemed to have a similar attitude towards classes, and their behavior didn’t influence me. Teachers made a difference—like in Accounting, where clear explanations and practical exercises helped me stay focused.

What Went Well & Challenges

  • What went well: Accounting went well because it was engaging, practical, and easier to study.
  • Negative effects: I struggled with Marketing because I lacked effort and motivation to study theoretical material.

Improvements for Sprint 2

What can I do differently?
I will study earlier and stick to a schedule. I need to rely on myself and create weekly goals as a first step to stay on track.

Summary of Lessons Learned

Attend all classes, start studying early, and stay consistent—even for subjects you find less interesting.

Learning Question

How can I stay motivated to study subjects I find boring and still perform well in them?
